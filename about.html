About yellow3 lab

What happens when people stop operating software and start directing systems?

yellow3 lab is an independent research and venture lab in Copenhagen, Denmark - studying how humans and increasingly autonomous systems work together.

The question

For most of the history of computing, software has been something people actively use. We click buttons, navigate interfaces, fill in forms, move data between systems, and manually execute workflows. Software has largely been a tool that requires continuous human operation.

We believe that is beginning to change.

Advances in artificial intelligence, automation, and digital infrastructure are making it possible for software to take on a growing share of execution. Increasingly, humans define goals, constraints, priorities, and judgment, while systems handle the work required to achieve an outcome.

This shift raises important questions.

  • How should these systems be designed?
  • What decisions should remain human?
  • How do organizations operate when software becomes increasingly autonomous?
  • How do trust, accountability, and governance evolve when execution is delegated to machines?

These questions sit at the center of yellow3 lab's work.

What we do

yellow3 lab is an independent research and venture lab based in Copenhagen, Denmark. We research how humans and increasingly autonomous systems work together, and how that relationship is reshaping software, organizations, markets, and society.

Our work focuses on the emerging discipline we call Outcome Architecture.

Outcome Architecture is the design of systems where humans define objectives, constraints, and judgment, while software increasingly executes the work required to achieve an outcome.

We believe this represents a fundamental shift in how digital systems are designed and how businesses operate. Just as the internet changed how information moved and cloud computing changed how software was delivered, autonomous systems are changing how work itself is performed.

Through essays, frameworks, field reports, and ongoing research, we seek to better understand the forces shaping this transition.

Research and practice

Research is only useful if it survives contact with reality.

Alongside our research activities, yellow3 lab builds and supports companies that put these ideas into practice. These ventures serve as real-world laboratories where concepts are tested against users, markets, regulation, operational constraints, and the complexities of everyday business.

This relationship matters. Research informs practice. Practice informs research. Ideas become stronger when they are challenged by reality, and products become better when they are grounded in clear thinking.

Rather than treating research and execution as separate disciplines, we see them as part of the same process.

Why we exist

Many conversations about artificial intelligence focus on models, tools, and technology. We are more interested in the systems that emerge around them.

Technology matters, but technology alone rarely changes the world. What matters is how people use it, how organizations adapt to it, how institutions respond to it, and how new forms of work become possible because of it.

Our goal is not simply to follow technological change. Our goal is to understand how that change reshapes the structures around us.

We believe the next generation of organizations will look fundamentally different from those that came before. We believe software will increasingly move from being something people operate to something people direct. And we believe understanding that transition will be one of the defining challenges of the coming decades.

We are not trying to predict the future. We are trying to understand how it is being built.
