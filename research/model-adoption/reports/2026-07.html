Executive summary

Five conclusions, three minutes. What changed across model routing, economics and the European picture in July 2026.

  1. Asia holds the lead at 60.6% of routed tokens (+6.3 pp over four weeks) - the concentration of demand by region of origin is not easing.
  2. Z.ai GLM 5 was the month's biggest climber, up 15 places to #29.
  3. No tracked price cuts landed inside the window this month.
  4. 3 new model(s) entered the ranking, led by Tencent Hy3 (free).
  5. Europe remains marginal at 0.51% of routed traffic; the regulatory picture is tracked in Europe Watch below.

Europe Watch

  • Routed share - European-built models hold 0.51% of routed traffic.
  • EU AI Act - Edition 02 · 13 July 2026: The Digital Omnibus became final: the Parliament adopted it on 16 June, the Council on 29 June, locking in the slide of high-risk obligations to 2027 and 2028. Only Official Journal publication remains. Fines issued stayed at zero.
  • Digital Product Passport - public call record 0-1; First call graded: the EU missed its own service-provider deadline. The registry's 19 July test is next.
  • Sovereignty - Mistral remains the only European developer in the routed top tier.

Europe Watch draws on yellow3's live EU AI Act and Digital Product Passport instruments.

Appendix

Methodology

Adoption is measured from OpenRouter routed-token traffic over trailing windows; region reflects developer headquarters. Pricing and capabilities are pulled from the OpenRouter public model feed and provider uptime. This is developer routing behaviour, not the whole market.

Tracked providers

Anthropic, DeepSeek, Google, MiniMax, Moonshot, NVIDIA, OpenAI, Poolside, StepFun, Tencent, Xiaomi, Z.ai.

Definitions

Routed share = a model's tokens as a percentage of all routed tokens in the window. Standard workload = 1,000 coding-agent tasks/month (100K input, 10K output, 70% cached).

Data

Underlying figures: model-adoption-data.json. CSV exports are a Professional feature.
