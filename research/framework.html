The method

We publish research to improve decisions, not to inform.

Every yellow3 lab report is built to one standard - the Decision Intelligence Framework. Seven layers, from signal to decision, ending on a single page a board can read in three minutes.

Turn complexity into decisions.
The standard

Every report answers four questions.

01

What is happening?

02

Why is it happening?

03

Why does it matter?

04

What should decision-makers do next?

The goal is not information. The goal is clarity.

The framework

Seven layers, from signal to decision.

Each layer turns raw complexity into something a leader can act on. This is the architecture - the detailed operating standard sits behind it.

01
SignalWhat is actually happening?
OutputA concise, factual picture of reality.
02
NoiseWhat is getting attention but matters less?
OutputA cleaner understanding of the landscape, with the hype filtered out.
03
EvidenceWhat supports the conclusions?
OutputA defensible foundation - data, sources, and where the uncertainty lies.
04
RiskWhat could go wrong?
OutputA practical understanding of the threats that could change the outcome.
05
OpportunityWhere is the advantage?
OutputActionable mapping of where early action creates an edge.
06
DecisionWhat should leadership actually do?
OutputClear, prioritised recommendations - for the CEO, the board, the investor.
07
WatchlistWhat should be monitored next?
OutputAn executive monitoring list - what would change the conclusions.
The board page

Every report ends on one page.

The Board Page distils the entire report into a single briefing - situation, stakes, risk, opportunity, and the recommended move.

A board member should be able to read it in under three minutes. Example below.

What ships

Every report ships as five executive artifacts.

01

Executive Brief

The whole report in two pages - readable in under five minutes.

02

CEO Brief

03

Board Page

The single-page briefing, with risk, opportunity, and the move.

04

Practical next steps, prioritised - not a framework, a to-do list.

05

Watchlist

The indicators to monitor before the next review.

The editorial standard

yellow3 lab does not chase headlines. It identifies signal.

It does not publish opinions without evidence. It takes positions when the evidence supports them.

Success is measured by the quality of decisions improved, not the volume of information produced.
