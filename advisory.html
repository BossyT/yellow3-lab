The reality

Every executive has an AI opinion.
Very few have a defensible one.

Vendors want your budget. Analysts want your attention. Your internal team wants to avoid being wrong. So the conversation becomes noise. Buzzwords. Pilot projects. Slide decks full of promises.

But almost nobody is willing to say the uncomfortable part out loud: what AI can actually do for a business today. And what it absolutely cannot.

Right now, the gap between AI reality and boardroom perception is widening every quarter. Some organisations are closing that gap - redesigning operations, compressing cost structures, and building capabilities competitors cannot easily copy. Others are approving multi-million budgets for technology they do not truly understand.

That difference will decide who leads the next decade.

Come prepared

Bring a real problem.

This is not a keynote. It is not a workshop with fifty people in the room.

This is a private, one-to-one session for top management or the business owner.

Before the session, you will be invited to submit a short description of a real challenge from your organisation. Something your team has been circling for months.

It might be a process that is slow, manual, or expensive. A decision that requires better data. A product or service idea that never gets resourced. A workflow that consumes time across multiple departments.

The kind of problem that everyone knows exists, but no one has had the time, clarity, or tools to properly solve.

During the session, we work on your problem. Directly. Together we break down the challenge, examine where AI can realistically help, and design a practical solution path. In many cases we will prototype the approach live, so you can see exactly how modern AI systems can be applied inside your business.

No generic frameworks. No theoretical discussions.

Just a clear view of what is possible, what is not, and what it would take to implement it.

You leave with more than inspiration. You leave with a concrete solution direction, a clearer understanding of where AI can create real leverage in your organisation, and a practical next step you can act on immediately.

Your submission should be simple. Up to 400 words. Describe the challenge, provide a bit of context, and explain what a good outcome looks like. That is all we need.

The goal is simple. Turn one real business problem into a real AI opportunity.

Thomas Chr. Melskens is a founder, operator, and researcher focused on how artificial intelligence is changing the relationship between humans, software, and organizations.

Over the past three decades, he has built companies, advised leadership teams, and worked across multiple industries. That experience shaped a simple observation: most business problems are not technology problems. They are decision-making problems, coordination problems, and execution problems.

As founder of yellow3 lab, Thomas researches what happens when people move from operating software to directing systems. Alongside the lab's research, he actively builds and operates AI-native products - so ideas are tested against real users, real markets, and real operational constraints rather than remaining theoretical exercises.

When he explains what AI can and cannot do, it does not come from analyst reports. It comes from building the systems himself.

Not how to build better tools. But how to build better systems.
